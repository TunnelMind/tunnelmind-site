Trust attestation layer for the agentic internet

When an agent acts on the open internet, can it prove the destination was trustworthy?

TunnelMind answers that in one signed call. An agent asks verify(destination); we fuse four independent lenses over one intelligence corpus and return a cross-lens verdict with an Ed25519 receipt and an attestation tier — built Claude-first, agent-native.

The moat

Anyone can buy a threat feed. The defensible asset is the join across four lenses on a single graph — and signed, witnessable receipts that make every verdict checkable after the fact.

Three pillars

Destination intelligence

Who and what is on the other end — across hostile-network, ad-supply, tracker, and routing signals.

Provenance

Every verdict is Ed25519-signed and hash-chained. The reasoning travels with the answer.

Witnessability

Receipts verify offline. A third party can re-check a decision without trusting us.
